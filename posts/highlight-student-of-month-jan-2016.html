Highlight Student Athlete of the Month

Macy Capen

Posted on 20th Feb, 2014 Categories: Uncategorized

Macy Capen, a four year member of the TCU Women’s Volleyball team and Speech-Language Pathology major, has been a dynamic leader for TCU FCA. After being the only volleyball player to attend the 2014 College Advance retreat, Macy returned to campus eager to serve her teammates and other girls through FCA. Since then, she’s joined our leadership team, regularly attended and contributed to the FCA Women’s Bible Study, and been appointed TCU FCA Co-President. Macy’s passion for service and Christ extended beyond TCU’s campus in 2015 when she founded Seven + Heart, an organization that turns recycled pillow cases into baby swaddles for orphans around the world. Seven + Heart recently sent swaddles with a team from Promise Child to Nepal, where they were wrapped around orphans affected by the 7.9 magnitude earthquake in April 2015.

While Macy prayed and worked diligently to found Seven + Heart, she also went through a battle of her own. After years of chronic back pain, doctors diagnosed Macy with spina bifida. She underwent a spinal fusion and a double laminectomy, ending her collegiate career in the fall of 2015. Through this hardship, we watched Macy cling fervently to the Word of the Lord, confident that “ He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion until the day of Christ Jesus” (Philippians 1:6. Her leadership never waivered, even through the fear and the struggles she faced. Macy shared her testimony with our FCA family this semester and boldly proclaimed that “if you know Christ as Savior, you’re testimony is powerful and worth sharing.”

An exceptional student, Macy graduated Cum Laude from TCU in December 2015, and plans on attending graduate school in Fall 2016. Her future goals include working at a pediatric hospital or opening a private practice, while also sharing the gospel through missions and Seven + Heart. We are so grateful for Macy’s life and how much she has contributed to our TCU FCA family. Her life speaks loudly, and we can’t wait to see where God takes her.
